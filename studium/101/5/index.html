The Personality

Presented by Dane Aleksander, art director at Animat Habitat.

Continued from The History. Part of the Animat Habitat studio workshop: Character Design for Animation.

Character is revealed through action in story, where we see how the character reacts to given situations. We use comic strips to excersize a range of character expressions that depict emotional highs and lows. We avoid having the character answer questions directly with dialogue, instead, we have a character respond as only the character would, and with considered timing in the response. We figure out what visceral passion drives a character, and consider making the character more selfish. We all have our own ambitions, desires, motivations and vulnerabilities and so on. And so too should a character in a story. We tend to share traits with a character that we create—evaluate the differences between a character that you create and yourself. We want to relate to a story, and to see ourself in story. We look at a character as the window into a story.
