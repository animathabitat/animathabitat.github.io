Photo 101: 1/6

The Audience

Presented by Dane Aleksander, art director at Animat Habitat.

Part of the Animat Habitat studio workshop: Character Design for Animation.

We begin to construct the shapes and colors that help to identify a character within its role in a story. We consider how an audience is likely to react to a character in the context of a story. We see how artistic style can also serve to describe a character, and that a character design echoes the approach that is taken to create it. Line quality, for example: heavy, rounded and soft strokes may suggest a calm quality in a character whereas sharper, rougher lines may be indicative of more erratic behaviour. We focus on a selection of key features for character design that have visual contrast, and that hold visual weight for a select audience.
