The History

Presented by Dane Aleksander, art director at Animat Habitat.

Continued from The Context. Part of the Animat Habitat studio workshop: Character Design for Animation.

We explore the history of a character to further its fiction. We outline the world in which a character exists, the part of that world where the character comes from, and the life-changing experiences that help support the authenticity of – and subsequent belief in – the character in a story. We examine dependencies between a character and a story, and see how the driving force that advances a character in story is revealed through action. We see that character flaws are often what make a character appealing, and how the discovery of incompleteness, or of goals, helps to create the dramatic thrust behind the adventures that a character is surely to embark upon. Character is a measurable journey. And we can measure the ultimate sacrifice of a character by a choice made at the beginning. We look to design a character that appears to exist outside of the box—the timeline that appears in story.
