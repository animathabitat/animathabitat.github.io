Inaugural Address

Halifax, NS Canada
Preceded by: Incorporation 2015

OAFA — Animat Habitat™ has accrued a cumulative operating loss through its financial year end of 2016 of ~CA$35,000 and, as expected, has expended a significant portion of the initial capital directed to advance the studio through its foundation and nearer to a first publication of wildlife art. This sum total has accounted for a select record of calculated investments, some of which predate the organization, all of which have made possible the progress of the organization to date. The goal is to direct investment in the organization to a computer art studio suite made to produce a library of scenes in wildlife art, which has a capacity to multiply the investment manifold given a market for constructive art in support of wildlife conservation. Studio projects take time and capital investment, and then exist in perpetuity, in many cases, in a digital art form that is transacted at little cost relative to the studio, to the environment, and to the audience and so on. Studio projects are in part designed to be made available as a public digital archive, to connect more and more people with the wild places and with stories of conservation. All revenue from the full release of the digital publication of studio projects is directed as a donation to wildlife conservation projects on the ground. The release of the first publication at the studio is designed to accomplish this very goal for the organization.

Investment at the studio may be categorized into four types of overhead cost: hardware, software, fees and development. Any expired resource such as a rented work space, a carrier network, a subscription service or software and so on is kept to a minimum. Any customized hardware – as well as licensed animation and design software – is perceived as a capital investment to produce commercial art at the studio, which in future may generate support for wildlife conservation. Computer art hardware and software is selected with consideration of its quality of design: its craftsmanship and its sustainability, to maximize its return on investment (to the benefit of projects in future). The hardware is also selected with consideration of its cost to manufacture and its use of materials: the material waste, the mining and use of rare-earth metals (oft-used for batteries, magnets, microchips and so on). Software architecture is tangibly less of a tax on the environment. So it goes.

In general, the hardware and software used for computer art at Animat Habitat™ is perceived as a proud contribution made on behalf of the organization to the companies that engineer such inspiring computer art tools. The investment in a computer art studio suite is like a contribution made in support of an inspiring artist or a work of art, and is justified in pursuit of that which has inspired the founding of the studio. There is also solace in the understanding that less attention need be paid to the figure for any single year, and that the net gains or losses over a longer term is of more significance to the advancement of the core mission: to save the wild places and the animals that live in them.

OAFA OFFICIAL WEBSITE — The official website of Animat Habitat™ was branched off of the project broadcast: art and design, and elephants, where project updates were shared under the umbrella of the portfolio website of Dane Aleksander (founder and interim president, public accountant and secretary and so on). In 2015, the Animat Habitat™ website was designed and developed with the open-sourced WordPress information architecture, and with a custom CSS theme based on the visual identity of the organization. The visual identity was described in detail, also in 2015, in an organizational document published as Animat Habitat™ canon, or corporate style guide or lookbook 01. Included in the visual identity is a transcription of the focus and tone of voice of the studio, which is echoed in language used in the documents of incorporation.

OAFA SCENE ONE — Animat Habitat™ scene one: White Elephant is in production. In 2015, the project has entered into the animation stage of the animated short film production; the project has a color script, character art, environment art, a story, technical direction for character animation, and a rough animatic with blocking, sequencing and pacing; the project has rendering, audio engineering and video compositing to come. This first publication at Animat Habitat™ has its digital release scheduled in the first quarter of 2020, and is made to be presented as a direct revenue stream for wildlife conservation in Africa

A limited edition print may then be made available to best represent the multimedia art in part in a tangible form. A print run may also be an opportunity for the studio to see a return on invested capital. From a financial perspective, however, this first project is recognized as a likely loss leader.

A more tragic loss. Long days at the studio here in Canada have been far too short when, at times in the making of White Elephant, in Africa: an elephant was being poached on average every fifteen minutes. For years, on average, nearly one hundred elephants were killed for their tusks every day! There was an estimated 350,000 elephants in the wild in 2013, and thjat year nearly 35,000 were killed, mostly to fuel an ivory trade that continues to fund a global criminal network, and to deprive Africa, and the world, of its nature and its treasure: wildlife. The fate of elephants in the wild is in our hands. /scene/one/…

This focus on the iconic animals: the keystone and umbrella species, is by popular demand. Saving the elephants in Africa is about protecting the habitats where elephants live, so that they and the animals that fall under the umbrella of ecosystems they create may continue to coexist. Saving places for wild animals is one issue, and ensuring their coexistence with ever-encroaching human communities is a second. The hope is to create a visual history of the natural world that may encourage people to appreciate the wilderness, to coexist with wildlife, to conserve a world with wild places. Animat Habitat™ is in production on a library of scenes in wildlife art to support the conservation of wild animals.


The public availability of financial records is reserved to members of Animat Habitat™ with the explicit purpose of maintaining a responsible and transparent practice of investment in computer art technology at the studio.
