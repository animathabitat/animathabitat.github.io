OTHER PROJECTS — An opportunity to collaborate on another project about elephants has been pressed upon the studio schedule, and the first publication at Animat Habitat™ has been pushed a minimum estimate of six-months — to fall of 2020.

This other project about elephants is a documentary film by James Currie, titled Last of the Big Tuskers. This documentary informs of the importance of dominant bulls in elephant social structures, with uniquely less of a focus on the role of matriarchs and the herds, and instead with a story that looks at the other elephant groups: askari bachelor herds. These elephant bulls, called askaris, follow in the footsteps of a patriarch: the biggest bull elephant (likely with the biggest tusks) in the area. The increasingly few elephants with tusks estimated in excess of one-hundred pounds per tusk are known as “super-tuskers”. This film aims to document the problem of when an elephant with the genetic makeup for big tusks has just reached the physical maturity of a “super-tusker”, that elephant has just reached prime age for breeding, however, at the same time, that elephant has just become a prime target for poaching. The film aims to explore the solution of managing the tourism market to support national parks, and the communities who continue to live on the front lines of a conflict with the wildlife that we the people – everywhere on earth – very clearly cherish and uphold in story and in art.
