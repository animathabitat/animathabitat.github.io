OAFA CONSERVATION—World Wildlife Fund (WWF) in Canada was identified as an official channel for revenue from the art of scenes two and three at Animat Habitat. The work of WWF to protect nature has elevated the identity of the organization to an international stage. The chapter of WWF in Canada has progressive leadership on environmental policy with Megan Leslie, and has held as a priority the conservation of coniferous forest habitat as well as arctic archipelago habitat. The studio has managed this channel to support the conservation of boreal forest in Canada as well as polar bear dens.

The studio has continued to follow the work of Sheldrick Wildlife Trust (SWT)—this year marked the graduation of Kiasa from her nursery herd in Nairobi to a reintegration unit in Tsavo East National Park. This year, SWT also commemorated two elephants who were part of the same nursery herd and who were lost along their journey back to the wild. The lionhearted Luggard had spent time with Kiasa at the nursery before being translocated to a reintegration unit in Umani Springs. There, despite his injured leg, Luggard was able to experience a semblance of a wild existence before he passed away. Kiasa and two age-mates, Kiombo and Maktao, were translocated to the same reintegration unit shortly after the passing of Luggard. Kiasa, Kiombo and Maktao are now thriving in Umani Springs. Two more age-mates, Maisha and Nabulu, were subsequently translocated to a second reintegration unit in Ithumba. Shortly thereafter, Maisha quite mysteriously and suddenly passed away. The loving labour of hand-raising elephant orphans carries on as the late Dame Daphne Sheldrick once described, “tempered with a good dose of tears.” More on this story at sheldrickwildlifetrust.org. ~/maisha ~/kiasa

The studio has continued to follow the photography of Nick Brandt—This Empty World (photography book, 2019) further explores the intersection of humans and wild animals. The book further raises awareness for the work of Big Life Foundation. This year, the studio has supported Big Life Foundation as they pressed against agricultural development in Kenya in the critical Kimana corridor for the greater Amboseli ecosystem. More on this story at biglife.org. ~/stop-work-order-on-kiliavo ~/avocado-farm-dismissed

The studio has signed on to a number of petitions. We registered with Stop Ecocide in support of the legal and practical assistance required to create the one law with the power to protect the future of life on earth. We registered with Environment America to protect the boreal forest from deforestation. We echo the statement of public lands campaign director Ellen Montgomery, “The boreal forest is home to billions of birds and endangered caribou and the idea that it is being logged at all, much less unsustainably, is tragic.” And, we registered with First People of Southern Africa (SAN) to protect the Okavango Delta watershed from crude oil drilling. This petition closed with 3,389 supporters. The undersigned supporters of SAN strongly oppose plans to prospect for, and potentially extract, oil and gas in the Kavango basin. /scene/one/elephant-conservation-kavango

OAFA IN CANADA—The studio has continued to follow MP Mumilaaq Qaqqaq, who has made the decision not to seek re-election and has delivered a farewell speech to the House of Commons.

“[…] I will always fight for human rights of Indigenous peoples in Nunavut and across the country. I believe that we are living through a shift […] where Canadians are starting to wake up to the reality. I am looking forward to a time where people like me can belong here. I hope another – young or Inuk or women, or all three – will follow in my footsteps and continue pushing this Institution to support Indigenous peoples in Canada.” — Mumilaaq Qaqqaq, member of Parliament for Nunavut delivers farewell speech in House of Commons, 15 June 2021

Mumilaaq was recognized as a principal member at Animat Habitat in 2020 for her progressive leadership. We have supported Mumilaaq Qaqqaq as a voice for Nunavut as well as for Canada, and we look forward to following wherever this young Inuk women will go next. Mat'na Mumilaaq. We thank her for a progressive term of public service. We stand with people across Canada in support of a future that upholds environmental protection and Indigenous rights. Register to vote. (Canada)
